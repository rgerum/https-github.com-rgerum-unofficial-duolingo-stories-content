Speaker3304: Tinianan also has 4 vowel marks, the acute accent (á) marks the stressed vowel following some rules that aren't important now, the grave acute (à) makes different sounds depending on the vowel, ò makes a less-deep u sound, à makes an ow sound like in the word "saw", ù makes an ü sound (again text-to-speech is unable to recreate it) and è makes an ea sound like in the word "pearl". The circumflex (â) marks long vowels, it can also be written with an h behind (ah) instead. The umlaut (ü) is used in the ï to make a weak i sound and in the ü it makes a wi sound.
