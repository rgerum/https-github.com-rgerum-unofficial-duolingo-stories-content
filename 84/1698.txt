> This will be your final lesson on the Telugu script. We just have one more lesson. In Telugu, each glyph represents one syllable. The first consonant is written as normal, but if there is a second or long consonants a second small symbol is written underneath. Most of these symbols are exactly the same as normal, minus the checkmark indicating a vowel (Example: గ+్+గ -> గ్గ). However there are some exceptions, in this lesson we will look at those. All double consonants will be represented using 'క'.
